# Player Manual

A quick reference for players in the D&D combat system.

## Combat Actions

On your turn you get **1 Action**, **1 Bonus Action** (if you have a feature that grants one), **Movement**, and **1 Reaction** per round.

### Actions (cost your Action)

| Action | What it does | Example prompt |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Attack** | Make a melee or ranged weapon attack against an enemy | "I attack the goblin with my longsword" |
| **Cast Spell** | Cast a spell using a spell slot (or a cantrip for free) | "I cast Fireball at the goblins" / "I cast Cure Wounds on Elara" |
| **Dodge** | Until your next turn, all attacks against you have disadvantage and you get advantage on DEX saves | "I dodge" |
| **Dash** | Double your movement this turn | "I dash toward the exit" |
| **Disengage** | Your movement doesn't provoke Opportunity Attacks this turn | "I disengage and back away" |
| **Help** | Give an ally advantage on their next attack against a specific enemy | "I help Thorin attack the goblin" |
| **Hide** | Make a Stealth check. Success = hidden; your next attack gets advantage | "I hide in the shadows" |
| **Ready** | Prepare an action with a trigger condition. Uses your Action now; fires as a Reaction when triggered | "I ready an attack for when the goblin comes within reach" |
| **Use Item** | Drink a potion, activate a magic item, etc. | "I drink my healing potion" |

### Reactions (1 per round, not per turn)

| Reaction | What it does | When it happens |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Opportunity Attack** | A free melee attack against an enemy leaving your reach | Automatic — triggered when an enemy moves away from you (unless they Disengage) |

### Other turn resources

- **Bonus Action** — Only available if a class feature, feat, or spell grants one (e.g. Rogue's Cunning Action = Dash/Disengage/Hide as Bonus Action)
- **Movement** — Move up to your speed. Can be split before and after your Action
- **Free Interaction** — Draw or sheathe a weapon, open a door, speak briefly

## Spellcasting

### How to cast a spell
Just say it naturally: *"I cast Fireball at the group of goblins"* or *"I use Magic Missile on the archer"*.

The DM will tell you which spell slot was used and ask for any required rolls.

### Spell slots
Each spell level has a limited number of uses per day. Cantrips (level 0) are unlimited.

| Slot Level | Example spells |
|-----------|---------------|
| Cantrip (0) | Fire Bolt, Sacred Flame, Mage Hand |
| 1st | Magic Missile, Cure Wounds, Thunderwave |
| 2nd | Hold Person, Misty Step, Scorching Ray |
| 3rd | Fireball, Counterspell, Hypnotic Pattern |
| 4th–9th | Higher-power versions of lower spells, or unique high-level spells |

### Saving throws
Some spells give enemies a chance to resist. The DM resolves enemy saves automatically. If an **enemy spell targets you**, the DM will ask you to roll a d20 saving throw — roll it on the dice panel.

- **Success** — You resist the worst effects (damage is often halved)
- **Failure** — Full effect applies

### Concentration
Some powerful spells require **concentration** to maintain. If you're concentrating:
- You can only concentrate on one spell at a time — casting a new concentration spell drops the old one.
- Taking damage forces a **Constitution saving throw** (DC = 10 or half damage, whichever is higher).
  - Success: keep concentration
  - Failure: the spell ends immediately

### Bonus Action spells
Some spells (e.g. Healing Word, Misty Step) cast as a **Bonus Action** — you can still use your Action for something else the same turn.

## Conditions

Conditions are status effects applied by spells, abilities, or combat events.

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Blinded** | Can't see; attack rolls against you have advantage, your attacks have disadvantage |
| **Charmed** | Can't attack the charmer; charmer has advantage on social rolls |
| **Frightened** | Disadvantage on attacks/checks while you can see the source of fear; can't willingly move closer |
| **Grappled** | Speed 0; can escape with a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check |
| **Incapacitated** | Can't take actions or reactions |
| **Invisible** | Can't be seen; attacks against you have disadvantage, your attacks have advantage |
| **Paralyzed** | Incapacitated, can't move or speak; attacks against you have advantage (and auto-crit if adjacent) |
| **Prone** | Disadvantage on attacks; attacks against you from more than 5 ft have disadvantage, adjacent attacks have advantage. Getting up costs half your movement |
| **Poisoned** | Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks |
| **Restrained** | Speed 0; attacks against you have advantage; your attacks have disadvantage |
| **Stunned** | Incapacitated, can't move; attacks against you have advantage |
| **Unconscious** | Incapacitated, can't move. Attacks against you have advantage, melee attacks auto-crit. See Death Saves |

## Death Saves

When a player reaches **0 HP**, they fall unconscious. At the start of each of their turns:

1. Roll a d20 on the dice panel (no modifiers).
2. **Natural 20** — Revive immediately with 1 HP.
3. **Natural 1** — 2 failures instead of 1.
4. **10 or higher** — Success.
5. **9 or lower** — Failure.

**3 successes** = Stabilized (no more saves needed, still unconscious).
**3 failures** = Dead.

Taking damage while unconscious adds an automatic failure (and a melee hit from 5 feet adds 2 failures).

## Tips

- **Rogue players**: You can Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a Bonus Action (Cunning Action), leaving your Action free to attack.
- **Surrounded and squishy?** Take the Dodge action to survive until help arrives.
- **Need to flee?** Disengage first (no free hits), then move. Or Dash if you're willing to risk the Opportunity Attack.
- **Want to set up an ally?** Help gives them advantage — great for enabling a Rogue's Sneak Attack.
- **Spellcaster running low on slots?** Cantrips are free — Fire Bolt, Sacred Flame, etc. always available.
- **Concentrating on a big spell?** Protect yourself from damage to maintain it — consider positioning or Dodge.
- **Not sure what to do?** Ask: *"What can I do?"* — the DM will explain your options. Or just say *"I end my turn"*.
